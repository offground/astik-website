Educational Philosophy

The Educational Philosophy of ASTIK

Every year, countless people visit adventure parks and ropes courses such as high ropes courses and zip-lines for recreation, or participate in youth development programs. These activities are highly effective in helping participants build confidence and a sense of achievement through the experience of taking on challenges and managing risk themselves.

However, achieving these positive outcomes requires safe and systematic operations. Creating an environment where participants can fully focus on their challenges depends on the professionalism of the operational staff — and that professionalism begins with structured training.

Under the slogan "Engineering Safety, Inspiring Adventure," ASTIK trains everyone involved in adventure parks and ropes courses to prioritize safety, so that participants can face their challenges with confidence.

ASTIK's training programs help operators thoroughly manage participant safety while inspiring participants to take on greater challenges. We believe that training for adventure parks and ropes courses should go beyond basic safety instruction — it should be a place where participants and operators grow and learn together.

* SBS (Self Belay System) — A system where the user manually operates connectors (carabiners) to attach to the safety line (EN 15567-1:2015, Category A/B)

** ISS (Interactive Safety System) — A system with two interlocking connectors where opening one automatically locks the other, ensuring continuous attachment (EN 15567-1:2015, Category C/D)

*** CBS (Continuous Belay System) — A system where the user remains permanently attached to the safety system during operation and cannot be detached without tools (EN 15567-1:2015, Category E)
